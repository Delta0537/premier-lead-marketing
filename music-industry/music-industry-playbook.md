# 1. INDUSTRY OVERVIEW

## The Modern Music Business Landscape

The music industry has fundamentally shifted from a gatekeeper-controlled model to a creator-economy model. Understanding this shift is critical to selling our services effectively.

### The Old Model (Pre-2010)
- Labels controlled distribution, promotion, and access
- Artists needed labels to reach audiences
- Revenue came primarily from album sales
- Radio play was the primary discovery mechanism
- Labels owned masters and took 80-90% of revenue

### The New Model (2020s+)
- Anyone can distribute music globally (DistroKid, TuneCore, etc.)
- Social media IS the new radio (TikTok, Instagram, YouTube)
- Revenue is diversified: streams, sync, merch, touring, production services
- Direct-to-fan relationships are the most valuable asset
- Artists CAN own everything - but need marketing expertise

### Why This Matters for Us

**The Gap We Fill:**
Independent artists and producers now have ACCESS but lack VISIBILITY. They can put music everywhere but can't get anyone to listen. Labels offer visibility but take ownership.

**We offer visibility WITHOUT ownership.**

# 2. CLIENT ARCHETYPES

**Profile:**

- Has skills but limited business/marketing knowledge
- Sells some beats on BeatStars/Airbit

# 3. PAIN POINTS & DESIRES

## Universal Pain Points (All Music Clients)

### Visibility Crisis
> "I make great music but nobody hears it"

- Streaming platforms have 100,000+ new tracks uploaded daily
- Algorithms favor established artists
- Playlist placement is opaque and competitive
- Social media reach is declining without paid promotion

### The Label Dilemma
> "Labels want too much for too little"

- Labels offer visibility but take 20-50% of revenue
- Multi-year contracts with limited exit options
- Loss of creative control
- Artists don't own their masters
- "360 deals" take from ALL revenue streams

### Technical Overwhelm
> "I'm a musician, not a marketer"

- Don't understand digital marketing
- Intimidated by ads, funnels, email
- Don't have time to learn
- Previous attempts at marketing failed
- Don't know what actually works

### Cash Flow Constraints
> "I can't afford to invest in marketing"

- Income is unpredictable
- Hesitant to spend on "maybe" results
- Have been burned by "music promoters"
- Bootstrap mentality

### Lack of Business Systems
> "Everything is in my head/DMs"

- No CRM, no follow-up systems
- Miss opportunities due to disorganization
- Can't scale because they ARE the bottleneck
- No recurring revenue, always hunting

# 6. DEAL STRUCTURES

## Creative & Flexible Pricing Models

Music clients often have cash flow challenges. Here are flexible deal structures:

---

### Model 1: Performance Partnership

**Best For:** Cash-strapped artists with high potential

**Structure:**
- Minimal setup fee: $500-1,500 (covers hard costs)
- Revenue share: 10-20% of GROWTH we generate
- Only applies to attributable revenue:
  - New bookings through our funnels
  - Beat sales from our campaigns
  - New production clients from our leads
- Does NOT apply to existing income

**Terms:**
- 6-month minimum commitment
- Clear attribution system (unique links, codes)
- Monthly reconciliation
- 30-day exit after minimum

**Why It Works:**
- Low barrier to entry
- We're invested in their success
- Builds trust and long-term relationship
- As they grow, we grow

**Protect Yourself:**
- Clear definition of "attributable"
- Minimum monthly commitment to your time
- Cap on percentage (optional)
- Right to convert to flat rate after X months

---

### Model 2: Hybrid (Retainer + Performance)

**Best For:** Artists with some budget, want accountability

**Structure:**
- Lower retainer: $500-1,000/month
- Plus performance bonus: 5-10% on growth
- Covers base services + incentive for results

**Why It Works:**
- Covers your time costs
- Client feels you're invested in results
- Scalable as they grow

---

### Model 3: Deferred Payment / Payment Plan

**Best For:** Artists with potential, expecting future income

**Structure:**
- Full setup: $3,000-5,000 value
- Pays: $500-1,000 upfront
- Balance: $500/month for 6 months
- OR balance due upon reaching revenue milestone

**Why It Works:**
- Gets them started
- Spreads cost over time
- Shows good faith

---

### Model 4: Revenue Milestone Deal

**Best For:** Betting on high-potential artists

**Structure:**
- We build full system
- Reduced upfront: $1,000
- Trigger payment when they hit milestones:
  - First $10K in bookings = $1,000 payment
  - First $25K = additional $1,500
  - First $50K = additional $2,500

**Why It Works:**
- True alignment of incentives
- They only pay when winning
- Can result in premium total fee

---

### Model 5: Equity/Points Partnership

**Best For:** Artists you really believe in, early stage

**Structure:**
- Reduced or free services
- X% of music catalog royalties
- OR equity in their LLC/brand
- Long-term partnership

**Why It Works:**
- High risk, high reward
- True partnership
- Can be significant if they blow up

**Caution:**
- Legal agreement essential
- Pick VERY selectively
- Have entertainment lawyer review

---

### Model 6: Value-First Trial

**Best For:** Skeptical prospects, proof-of-concept

**Structure:**
- 30-day trial at reduced rate ($500)
- Specific deliverable (one funnel, one campaign)
- Measure results
- If successful, move to full engagement

**Why It Works:**
- Low risk for them
- Proves your value
- Builds trust fast

# 8. SALES CONVERSATIONS

## Discovery Questions for Music Clients

### Understanding Their Business
1. "Walk me through how you make money from music today."
2. "What's your split between streaming, sales, bookings, and production work?"
3. "What does a good month look like financially? A bad month?"
4. "What percentage of your income comes from referrals vs. your own marketing?"

### Understanding Their Goals
5. "Where do you want to be in 12 months?"
6. "If everything worked out perfectly, what does success look like?"
7. "What would need to happen for you to do music full-time?"

### Understanding Their Pain
8. "What's the most frustrating part of promoting your music?"
9. "What have you tried before that didn't work?"
10. "What's holding you back from the growth you want?"

### Understanding Their Situation
11. "Are you talking to labels? What are they offering?"
12. "What's your hesitation with those deals?"
13. "How much time are you spending on marketing vs. creating?"
14. "What tools or platforms are you currently using?"

### Budget Qualification
15. "What are you currently investing in marketing/promotion?"
16. "Have you thought about what you'd be willing to invest if you knew it would work?"
17. "Is it more important to keep costs low or to share upside on results?"

---

## The "Invisible Tax" Pitch

Use this when they're considering label deals:

> "Here's what I call the invisible tax. Right now, it seems like a label deal is 'free' because they're not asking for money upfront. But let's do the math.
>
> If you sign a deal that takes 30% of your streaming revenue, and over the next 10 years you earn $500,000 from those streams, you just paid $150,000 for marketing that you could have done yourself - or paid us a fraction of that to build for you.
>
> The label isn't investing in you. They're lending you visibility at loan-shark rates. What if instead, you invested $10K-20K over the next year building your OWN visibility machine that you own forever?"

# 11. INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

### Essential Terms

**A&R (Artists & Repertoire):** Label executives who discover and develop artists

**Advance:** Money paid upfront by a label (recouped from royalties)

**Beat Lease:** Licensing a beat to multiple artists (non-exclusive)

**Catalog:** An artist's complete body of work

**Distribution:** Getting music on streaming platforms

**Drop:** Releasing new music

**EPK (Electronic Press Kit):** Digital portfolio for bookings/press

**Exclusive (Beat):** One-time sale of a beat to single artist

**Feature:** Guest appearance on another artist's track

**Ghost Production:** Producing without credit

**Label Services:** Label-like support without ownership

**Masters:** Original recordings (who owns these = who controls them)

**Mechanical Royalties:** Paid when music is reproduced/streamed

**Mix:** DJ set or audio engineering process

**Performance Royalties:** Paid when music is played publicly

**Placement:** Getting a beat on a released song

**Points:** Percentage points of royalties

**Pre-Save:** Fans saving an upcoming release before it drops

**Publishing:** Ownership of the composition (separate from recording)

**Recoupable:** Costs deducted from artist royalties

**Residency:** Ongoing DJ booking at a venue

**Splits:** Dividing royalties among collaborators

**Stems:** Individual tracks of a recording

**Sync (Licensing):** Music used in TV, film, ads, games

**Type Beat:** Instrumental mimicking a famous artist's style

**360 Deal:** Label takes percentage of all revenue (not just music)

---

- **BeatStars / Airbit:** Beat selling platforms
- **DistroKid / TuneCore / CD Baby:** Distribution services
